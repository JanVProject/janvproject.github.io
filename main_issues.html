Main issues for Croatian children in foster homes and foster parents

As of today, 4439 children are without appropriate parental care.

2133 - Foster homes

2306 - Foster families

Graphical representation of the distribution of children between foster families and foster homes

In comparison to other developed countries, this percentage of children in foster homes (to total number of children in the foster care system) is high.

Problems for children

Lengthy stay in foster homes

Children arrive at foster homes very young, but many end up spending their entire childhoods in foster homes because of sometimes complicated proceedings and situations.

Children have no place in institutions in the long run and this should just be a stopover.

Many Croatian foster homes are overcrowded

Lack of privacy, a safe place to think, study and develop due to an ever so growing number of children in foster homes.

Lack of family environment, closeness and stability.

Family is an irreplacable factor for children. Even though social care workers try to emulate a comfortable enviornment, many children lack stability and closeness and many times develop with different psychological problems and lackings.

Long legal proceedings

Many times legal proceedings of deprivation of parental care can take more than 5 years, leaving children in foster homes exhausted and traumatisied by their parents' actions.

Children are not the imperative in legal proceedings.

Often, children and their wishes are often disregarded and their parents are set as the imperative.

Chance for chronic offences

According to many different statistics, young people who spent a significant period in foster homes are much more likely to commit offenses than those who do not.

Problems for foster parents

Complexity and longevity of the process

Possible foster parents often get frightened by the process of becoming official foster parents due to its incredible complexity and longevity of process. Collecting, handling and delivering correct forms and documents can take months.

Very small fees

In comparison to other developed EU countries, fees for fostering in Croatia are very small. Additionally, with rising inflation each year, these already small fees seem to diminish incredibly quickly.

Even though many of these issues can only be solved by the Government and other institutions, Croatian citizens can play a major role in solving these issues.

The long term solution to these issues is educating yourself, others and interesting in fostering.
